About IEEE

IEEE is the world’slargest professional association dedicated to advancing technological innovation and excellence for the benefit of humanity. IEEE and its members inspire a global community through its highly cited publication, conferences, technology standards, and professional and educational activities. It has more than 426,000 members in more than 160 countries. IEEE was formed in 1963 after a merger between the Institute of Radio Engineers(IRE), founded in 1912, and American Institute of Electrical Engineers(AIEE).

IEEE is now the leading authority on a range of technology including aero space system, computers, telecommunication, biomedical engineering, electric power,consumer electronics and many more. The IEEE serves more than to just provide a networking platform for professionals. It sponsors more than 1,600 conferences in 91 countries annually to aid the progression to technology. With 2,669 student branches at colleges and universities in 80 countries, it has a membership population of nearly 117,000 students.

About Women In Engineering(WIE) Society

IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE) is an international professional organisation dedicated to supporting women engineers and scientists. WIE strives to inspire young women across the world to pursue their academic interests and build a career in engineering. WIE facilitates the recruitment and retention of women in technical disciplines globally. WIE envisions a vibrant community of determined IEEE women and men collectively using their diverse talents to innovate for the benefit of humanity.

IEEE WIE strives to: Recognise the outstanding achievements of women in electrical and electronics engineering through IEEE Awards nominations. Organise receptions at major technical conferences to enhance networking and to promote membership in IEEE WIE. Advocate women in leadership roles in IEEE governance, and career advancement for women in the profession. Facilitate the development of programs and activities that promote the entry into, and retention of, women in engineering programs.

About IEEE Computer Society

The scope of the Society encompasses all aspects of theory, design, practice, and application relating to computer and information processing science and technology. The IEEE Computer Society is the world's leading membership organization dedicated to computer science and technology. Serving more than 60,000 members, the IEEE Computer Society is the trusted information, networking, and career-development source for a global community of technology leaders that includes researchers, educators, software engineers, IT professionals, employers, and students.

In addition to conferences and publishing, the IEEE Computer Society is a leader in professional education and training. It has over 400 local and regional chapters as well as more than 40 technical communities. IEEE CS also recognizes the field's pioneers and visionaries through a robust award program and work to support the next generation of technology leaders through our student scholarships. The IEEE Computer Society is the community for technology leaders. As the computing professional's single, unmatched source for technology information, inspiration and collaboration, IEEE CS is the organization that computing leaders, innovators, and researchers trust.

About IEEE NITK Student Branch

IEEE NITK is an award-winning student branch that belongs to the Bangalore Section under Region 10 of IEEE. Founded in 1988, NITK IEEE has progressed exponentially and involved itself in various regional and global technical activities. Winners of the Best Student Branch Award in 2007 and 2013, IEEE NITK is now one of the largest student branches worldwide.

IEEE NITK is comprised by four Special Interest Groups, namely:

      CompSoc
      CAS (Circuits and Systems Society)
      SPS (Signal Processing Society)
      Piston
      WIE (Women in Engineering)
      SIGHT (Special Interest Group on Humanitarian Technology)
